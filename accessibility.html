        Digital inclusion

        Designed to work for more people

        We aim for content that is understandable, keyboard-friendly, responsive, compatible with assistive technology and presented with meaningful structure and contrast.

        Navigate

        Keyboard and structure

        Pages use headings, landmarks, labels, visible focus and logical link text to support keyboard and screen-reader navigation.

        Perceive

        Images and media

        Meaningful images should include useful alternative text, while decorative images should not distract assistive technology.

        Tell us

        Report a barrier

        Accessibility improves through feedback. Tell us the page, feature, device and difficulty you experienced.

        Send accessibility feedback
        Using the site

        Practical accessibility support

        You can enlarge text using your browser zoom, navigate interactive elements with the keyboard and use built-in operating-system accessibility settings. The design is intended to adapt across desktop, tablet and mobile screens.

        Some older documents, third-party maps, social media embeds or externally supplied content may not yet meet the same standard. Where possible, we will provide an accessible alternative or the information in another format.

        When reporting a problem, include the page address, what you were trying to do and the browser or assistive technology used. We will review the issue and prioritise fixes according to impact and available resources.

        Need another format?

        Ask for information in a clearer, larger, spoken or otherwise accessible format. We will make reasonable efforts to help.
